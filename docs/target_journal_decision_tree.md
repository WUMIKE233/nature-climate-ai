# Target journal decision tree

## Start with the evidence, not the preferred title

1. Does the minimum evidence slice show a strong precursor signal?
   - No: do not continue Nature/Science drafting; revise the scientific question or target a methods/pilot venue.
   - Yes: continue to global scaling and external validation.

2. Does the full analysis show global or near-global recurrence across multiple biomes and years?
   - Yes: Nature remains plausible.
   - No: consider Nature Climate Change, Nature Ecology & Evolution, Global Change Biology or Remote Sensing of Environment.

3. Is the final result one crisp, high-impact discovery rather than a broad atlas?
   - Yes: Science may be a stronger target.
   - No: Nature or a Nature-family specialist journal may fit better.

4. Does independent ecosystem validation support the remote-sensing signal?
   - Yes: maintain main-journal positioning.
   - No: reduce claim strength and target a more specialized journal unless another independent validation source is available.

5. Are code, data access, checksums and reproducibility records complete?
   - No: submission remains blocked.
   - Yes: proceed to presubmission enquiry or final formatting.

## Title logic

Nature-style title options should foreground broad scientific discovery:

- "Globally recurrent climate precursors of ecosystem vegetation stress revealed by interpretable AI"
- "Compound heat-drought precursor pathways predict global vegetation stress"

Science-style title options should foreground one compact finding:

- "Compound climate precursors anticipate global vegetation stress"
- "AI reveals early-warning pathways for global vegetation stress"

## 中文审阅说明

目标期刊应由证据强度决定，而不是由愿望决定。若结果不能跨区域、跨年份、跨生态系统稳定成立，就应主动下调到更合适的期刊。
