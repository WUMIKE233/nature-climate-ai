# Nature claim strategy

## Selected guide additions

The guide's strongest recommendation is to position the project as a verifiable scientific discovery, not an AI system demonstration. This project will therefore treat AI as a discovery instrument and will promote a manuscript claim only if validated evidence supports all three layers:

- Predictive layer: lagged climate states anticipate vegetation-stress events with measurable lead time.
- Mechanistic layer: the strongest precursors are interpretable in terms of soil moisture, atmospheric water demand, heat and precipitation deficits.
- Generalization layer: the signal survives temporal holdout, spatial holdout and biome or sensor robustness checks.

## Working discovery claim

Use this only as a target claim until evidence exists:

> We identify globally recurrent climate precursor pathways that anticipate vegetation stress weeks to months before peak ecosystem response, and test whether compound heat-drought interactions improve predictive skill beyond conventional drought or temperature indicators alone.

The second clause is intentionally written as a test. It must become a positive claim only after baseline, ablation, uncertainty and external-validation artifacts support it.

## Claim promotion rule

- Keep the reserved result placeholder in the manuscript until the linked evidence item is complete.
- Describe unvalidated outputs as candidate precursors, hypotheses or pilot findings.
- Do not use causal language unless the analysis supports mechanism and plausible alternative explanations have been tested.
- Report negative or non-generalizing results instead of filtering them out.
