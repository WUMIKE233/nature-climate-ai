# Editorial significance rationale

## Central editorial question

Why would this belong in Nature or Science rather than a specialist climate, ecology, remote-sensing or AI journal?

The answer cannot be "because the model uses AI." The answer must be that the analysis reveals a broadly important climate-ecology pattern that changes how vegetation stress is anticipated, explained or validated.

## Nature threshold

Nature becomes plausible only if the evidence supports:

- global or near-global coverage over multiple decades,
- recurrent precursor pathways across multiple biomes or continents,
- lead time before satellite-visible vegetation stress,
- improvement over conventional climate indices and simple baselines,
- interpretable compound heat-drought mechanisms,
- and independent ecosystem-function validation, preferably through FLUXNET or another flux/functional dataset.

## Science threshold

Science becomes plausible if the final result is narrower but exceptionally crisp:

- one central early-warning pathway,
- a strong lead-time or compound-interaction finding,
- a decisive falsification of simpler single-variable explanations,
- and a compact story that can be told with few main display items.

## Redirect rule

If the evidence is solid but mainly regional, methodological or incremental, redirect away from Nature/Science main titles toward a better-matched venue such as Nature Climate Change, Nature Computational Science, Nature Ecology & Evolution, Global Change Biology, Remote Sensing of Environment or Environmental Research Letters.

## 中文审阅说明

本文件只回答一个问题：为什么这个结果值得正刊，而不是子刊或领域期刊。若最终结果不能证明全球重要性、机制解释、强基线优势和独立验证，就不应强行投 Nature/Science 正刊。
