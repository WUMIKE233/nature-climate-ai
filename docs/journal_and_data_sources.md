# Journal and data-source constraints

## Journal target

Primary target: Nature Article.

Practical implication: the manuscript should stay short, evidence-dense and broad in significance. The main text should carry only the central discovery and validation chain; data-processing details, robustness checks and extra regional results should move to Methods or Supplementary Information.

Fallback targets if the evidence is strong but narrower than Nature main:

- Science
- Nature Climate Change
- Nature Computational Science
- Science Advances

## Official source links to verify before submission

- Nature initial submission guidance: https://www.nature.com/nature/for-authors/initial-submission
- Nature formatting guide: https://www.nature.com/nature/for-authors/formatting-guide
- Nature presubmission enquiries: https://www.nature.com/nature/for-authors/presub
- Science/AAAS submission and manuscript-template access point: https://cts.sciencemag.org/
- Copernicus ERA5 climate reanalysis overview: https://climate.copernicus.eu/climate-reanalysis
- NASA MODIS vegetation indices product page: https://modis.gsfc.nasa.gov/data/dataprod/mod13.php
- FLUXNET2015 dataset page: https://fluxnet.org/data/fluxnet2015-dataset/

## Verification policy

Before submission, refresh all journal instructions and data-provider terms. These pages can change, and the manuscript package should not rely on stale length limits, data policies or formatting requirements.
