# Literature positioning for a Nature/Science submission

## Core research gap

The proposed paper should not claim novelty from using AI, MODIS or ERA5 alone. Those components are now standard. The stronger gap is:

> Existing global studies quantify drought, heat, soil-moisture or vapour-pressure-deficit impacts on vegetation, but few provide an interpretable, globally validated discovery framework for lagged compound precursor states that can be tested against independent ecosystem-function observations.

The manuscript should therefore emphasize discovery, validation and mechanism, not model complexity.

## Competitive landscape

### Earth-system AI and interpretability

Reichstein et al. argued that data-driven Earth-system science needs deep learning plus process understanding, not black-box prediction alone. This project should cite that framing and show how the proposed workflow uses AI to generate testable ecological hypotheses rather than only higher predictive accuracy.

Positioning implication: the Methods and Results must include interpretable attribution maps, lag-response evidence and failure cases.

### Soil moisture, VPD and plant water stress

Recent studies show that soil moisture and atmospheric water demand are central to ecosystem productivity stress. For example, global work has found soil moisture to be a dominant dryness-stress control over large areas of vegetated land, while newer work estimates global critical soil-moisture thresholds for plant water stress.

Positioning implication: the paper must not merely rediscover that soil moisture or VPD matters. It must show when, where and how early compound states become predictive before satellite greenness declines.

### Compound drought-heat extremes

Compound dry-hot extremes are already linked to productivity loss, vegetation resilience and ecosystem risk. Recent 2026 work also emphasizes escalating vegetation impacts from soil-based compound dry-hot extremes.

Positioning implication: the novelty should be framed around precursor discovery and independent validation, not around the existence of compound dry-hot impacts.

### Ecological forecasting

Near-term ecological forecasting is becoming a climate-action priority, and recent work shows that machine-learning approaches can predict abrupt shifts in regional dryland functioning.

Positioning implication: this manuscript needs a clear early-warning claim. Metrics should include lead time, calibration and operational usefulness, not only retrospective classification accuracy.

## Nature-first editorial thesis

Nature is plausible only if the analysis supports a broad statement like:

> A globally recurrent but regionally differentiated set of compound climate precursor states explains and predicts vegetation stress before satellite-visible decline, and these states are independently reflected in ecosystem flux anomalies.

This thesis requires all of the following:

- Global or near-global coverage over multiple decades.
- Clear lead time before visible vegetation stress.
- Improvement over standard climate indices and simple baselines.
- Cross-region transfer, not only random train/test splits.
- Independent ecological validation through FLUXNET or an equivalent source.
- A mechanistic interpretation grounded in climate-ecosystem processes.

## Science alternative thesis

Science may be a better target if the result is exceptionally crisp but more compact, for example:

> A small set of interpretable compound climate precursors predicts ecosystem stress across biomes with measurable lead time and independent flux-site validation.

Science positioning should be terser, with a sharper abstract and a stronger emphasis on a single central result. If the final analysis yields one striking mechanism or threshold rather than a broad global atlas, the Science route may become more attractive than Nature.

Use this as a seed list for targeted literature review, not as a final bibliography.

- Reichstein, M. et al. Deep learning and process understanding for data-driven Earth system science. *Nature* 566, 195-204 (2019).
- Hersbach, H. et al. The ERA5 global reanalysis. *Quarterly Journal of the Royal Meteorological Society* 146, 1999-2049 (2020).

- Huete, A. et al. Overview of the radiometric and biophysical performance of the MODIS vegetation indices. *Remote Sensing of Environment* 83, 195-213 (2002).
- Pastorello, G. et al. The FLUXNET2015 dataset and the ONEFlux processing pipeline for eddy covariance data. *Scientific Data* 7, 225 (2020).
