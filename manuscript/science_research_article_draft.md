## Introduction

Compound heat and drought increasingly threaten terrestrial ecosystem function. Standard drought and heat metrics track important hazards, but they often compress multi-variable climate stress into retrospective indices. A central open question is whether the climate states that precede visible vegetation stress can be discovered, interpreted and validated at global scale.

Artificial intelligence can search across high-dimensional lagged climate features, yet prediction alone is not enough for ecological inference. A useful discovery framework must show where a precursor state occurs, how early it appears, whether it transfers across regions and whether it corresponds to independent changes in ecosystem function.

Here we combine global satellite vegetation anomalies, climate reanalysis and eddy-covariance flux observations to test whether interpretable AI can discover compound precursor states before persistent vegetation stress. The central result is RESULT_REQUIRED.
