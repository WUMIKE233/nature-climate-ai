Ecological indicators for satellite-observed vegetation stress: a shared-grid framework across Australia (2000-2025)

1. Soil-moisture deficit and VPD excess at 16-32 day leads are the dominant climate precursor indicators of MODIS EVI vegetation stress across Australia.

2. A persistence indicator (previous stress event) achieves F1=0.660, substantially exceeding all climate-only precursor indicators (best: VPD family, F1=0.115).

3. Climate-only precursor indicators generate false-alarm ratios exceeding 0.94; machine learning (Random Forest, XGBoost) reduces this to approximately 0.81 but never below 0.80.

4. Spatial transfer of climate precursor indicators across 20 Australian grid regions is modest, with region-level precision rarely exceeding 0.12, and latitude-zone stratification reveals stronger climate-vegetation coupling in the subtropical interior.

5. A shared-grid audit framework ensures MODIS and ERA5-Land indicators are constructed on identical pixel grids, eliminating spatial-misalignment artefacts and enabling reproducible ecological indicator evaluation.
