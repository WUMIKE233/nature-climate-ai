# Editorial strategy: Nature versus Science

## Use Nature first if the final result has breadth

Choose Nature if the validated analysis supports a broad global discovery:

- recurring compound precursor states across multiple continents or biomes,
- clear early-warning lead time,
- a mechanism-level interpretation beyond pure prediction,
- independent FLUXNET support,
- and an evidence chain that matters to climate science, ecology and AI-for-science readers.

Preferred Nature title shape:

- "AI-guided discovery of compound climate precursors for global vegetation stress"
- "Compound climate precursors of global vegetation stress revealed by interpretable AI"

## Use Science first if the final result has a sharper single punchline

Choose Science if the validated analysis produces one compact result:

- one robust precursor mechanism,
- one strong threshold or lead-time finding,
- or a concise global test that decisively changes how ecosystem stress should be forecast.

Preferred Science title shape:

- "Compound climate precursors predict global vegetation stress"
- "An interpretable AI early-warning signal for vegetation stress"

## Desk-rejection risks

- The paper only shows that AI predicts NDVI/EVI better than baselines.
- The paper rediscovers known drought, soil-moisture or VPD controls without new timing or mechanism.
- The model is hard to interpret or fails spatial transfer.
- FLUXNET validation is missing or weak.
- The central claim depends on unverified causal language.
- The manuscript overstates results before evidence artifacts exist.

## Submission sequence

1. Complete the evidence registry.
2. Prepare a one-page presubmission enquiry for Nature if the result is broad enough.
3. If Nature declines after enquiry or desk review, convert to the Science draft only if the result is concise and decisive.
4. If the evidence is strong but narrower, redirect to Nature Climate Change, Nature Computational Science or Science Advances.
